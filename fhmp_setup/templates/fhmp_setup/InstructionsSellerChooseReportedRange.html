After each seller receives the number #, the seller does two things. First, the seller chooses to report a range of numbers to let buyers know what their asset value is likely to be. We will call the range the seller reports to buyers the Reported Range. In other words, the seller cannot tell buyers exactly what # is; they can only tell buyers a range of numbers.

Second, the seller chooses whether to make their Reported Range less or more precise. A less precise Reported Range is 5-numbers wide (for example 4-8) while a more precise Reported Range is only 3 numbers wide (for example 5-7).

Here are two important points:

  1. The seller must pay 2 points if they choose to report the more precise (3-number) range. The seller does not pay any points (0 points) if they choose to report the less precise (5-number) range.
  2. The seller can report any range. That is, the seller's Reported Range does not have to contain the number # they were given. For example, if # is 10, a seller could report a range of 0-5 or 8-12 or 16-20.

In summary, the seller does two things each round:

  1. Pick a precision: after seeing #, the seller can choose to report a range that is 5 numbers long (this is free) or 3 numbers long (this costs the seller 2 points).
  2. Report a range: after picking a precision, the seller can pick any range to report to buyers.
